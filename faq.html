Trading

A GTT order stays active for up to 365 days and fires automatically when the market price hits your trigger price.

1
Open the stock you want from your Watchlist.
2
Click the GTT button (clock icon) next to Buy/Sell.
3
Enter your trigger price and limit price.
4
Confirm. The GTT order is now active. You can view it under Orders → GTT.

Basket orders let you group up to 10 stocks across Equity and F&O and execute them simultaneously.

1
Go to Basket Orders from the left sidebar menu.
2
Click Create Basket and add stocks using the search.
3
Set quantity and side (Buy/Sell) for each stock.
4
Click Execute Basket to place all orders at once.

Cover Order (CO): Places a market order with a compulsory stop-loss. Offers higher intraday leverage.

Bracket Order (BO): Combines an entry order with a stop-loss and a target price. The system exits automatically at whichever triggers first.

Both are available only during market hours and are squared off by 3:15 PM if not triggered.

  • MIS (Margin Intraday Square-off): Used for intraday trading. Orders are squared off on the same day. Offers leverage.
  • CNC (Cash and Carry): Used for delivery trading. You must pay 100% of the trade value and hold shares in your demat for T+1 days.

  • SL (Stop Loss Limit): Requires both a Trigger Price and a Limit Price. Once the trigger is hit, a limit order is sent.
  • SL-M (Stop Loss Market): Only requires a Trigger Price. Once hit, a market order is sent, ensuring execution during high volatility.

An Iceberg order allows you to divide a large quantity order into smaller slices. Only the first slice is visible to the market, hiding the total depth.

Note: Minimum order value for Iceberg is ₹1,00,000 for Equity and ₹5,0,00,000 for F&O.

Portfolio & Margin

Intraday (MIS) orders allow you to trade with up to 5x leverage for Equity and 1x for F&O, as per SEBI mandates.

Note: MIS orders are automatically squared off by the system 15 minutes before market close.

MTF allows you to carry forward your leveraged positions for more than 1 day by paying a nominal interest rate.

1
Select MTF as the product type in your buy order.
2
Authorize the MTF pledge via CDSL e-DIS before 9:00 PM on T-day.
3
Maintain the required maintenance margin to avoid liquidation.

Indiabulls Securities shows P&L in two places:

  • Positions → real-time MTM (Mark-to-Market) P&L for open intraday trades.
  • Holdings → unrealised P&L for delivery stocks (CNC).

MTM = (Current Price − Buy Price) × Quantity. It updates live during market hours.

Pledging & Collateral

Pledging is the process of marking a lien on your shares with the depository (CDSL) to receive collateral margin for F&O or MTF trading. Shares remain in your Demat account, and you continue to receive dividends.

Indiabulls charges ₹35 per scrip (per ISIN) + GST for both pledging and unpledging, irrespective of the quantity. Margin is credited within 15 minutes during market hours.

A haircut is a risk-based discount. For example, if you pledge shares worth ₹1,0,000 with a 30% haircut, your usable margin will be ₹70,000. Haircuts are set by the exchange based on volatility.

You can unpledge your shares by visiting the Portfolio → Pledged Holdings section. Select the stock and click Unpledge. The shares will be available for selling or transfer after T+1 day.

Tender Offers (Buybacks & OFS)

A Tender Offer is a formal invitation by a company to shareholders to sell their shares at a specified price (usually a premium) for actions like Buybacks or Offer for Sale (OFS).

Go to the IPO / Tender Offer section in the app, view active lists, and enter your bid quantity. Your shares will be blocked until the settlement process is complete.

Futures & Options

CE (Call Option): Gives you the right to buy an asset at a strike price. Buy CE if you expect the market to rise.
PE (Put Option): Gives you the right to sell an asset at a strike price. Buy PE if you expect the market to fall.

  • Lot Size: Minimum quantity per contract (e.g., Nifty = 75).
  • Expiry: Date when the contract settles (Weekly/Monthly).
  • Strike Price: The price at which you can exercise the option.

A stock enters the ban period when its open interest exceeds 95% of the aggregate limit. During this time, you can only square off existing positions; no new ones are allowed.

To trade in Futures & Options, you need to enable the segment by uploading one of the following income proofs:

  • Last 6 months Bank Statement
  • Latest Salary Slip
  • ITR Acknowledgement
  • Demat Holding Statement (value > ₹10,000)

Once submitted, activation takes 24-48 working hours.

Margin Trading Facility (MTF)

MTF allows you to buy shares by paying only a fraction of the total cost (the margin), while Indiabulls funds the rest. These shares are automatically pledged as security for the funding.

MTF is available to all resident individual investors with active trading accounts. NRI, Minor, and Corporate accounts are currently not eligible for MTF funding.

Initial Public Offerings (IPO)

An IPO (Initial Public Offering) is when a company sells its shares to the public for the first time. This transition allows the company to raise capital from public investors while providing them with an ownership stake.
